Frequently Asked Questions

What kinds of opportunities are available in the club?

We offer a variety of roles including executive positions, director roles, volunteer opportunities, and mentorship programs. Each provides unique ways to get involved and grow your skills.

How can I apply for executive or director roles?

Applications open at the beginning of each semester. Details are posted on our Instagram, mailing list, and website. We encourage students from all years and backgrounds to apply.

Do I need prior experience to apply?

Not necessarily! While some leadership roles may prefer prior involvement, passion, commitment, and willingness to learn are the most important qualities we look for.

What do volunteers do?

Volunteers support events, outreach, and communications. It’s a flexible, low-commitment way to contribute while gaining valuable experience and meeting like-minded peers.

What is the mentorship program about?

Our mentorship program connects students with experienced mentors who provide guidance on research, academics, and career pathways in genetics and synthetic biology.

Can first-year or international students join?

Absolutely! We value diverse perspectives. First-year and international students are encouraged to get involved through volunteering, mentorship, or applying for team roles.

How much time commitment is expected?

Time commitment varies by role. Executive and director positions may require 3–5 hours per week, while volunteers and mentees can participate more flexibly depending on availability.

Where can I learn about open opportunities?

All open positions and programs are announced on our Instagram, mailing list, and the opportunities page of this website.
